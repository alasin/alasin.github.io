I am presently working as a Project Assistant at Visual Analytics Lab, Indian Institute of Science with Prof. R. Venkatesh Babu. We are working on deep learning approaches for monocular depth estimation, portrait segmentation and object dynamics transfer. Prior to this, I spent two great years working at Tonbo Imaging as a member of its computer vision R&D team. I worked on multiple multi-sensor imaging systems, most of which have been deployed on-field.

I completed my Bachelor's in Computer Science from BITS Pilani, India. Through my undergraduate projects and internships at Forus Health and Tonbo Imaging, I have developed a great interest in computer vision, especially in an applied research setting.

I am also a FOSS enthusiast. During my undergrad years, I successfully participated in Season of KDE (2014), and Google Summer of Code (2014 and 2015). I have since been a regular open-source contributor and have served as a mentor for Google Code-In, Season of KDE and Learn IT, Girl.

When I'm not working, I spend my time reading tech blogs, playing video games, watching thrillers and solving puzzles.
